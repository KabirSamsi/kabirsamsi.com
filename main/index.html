I am a third-year undergraduate studying Computer Science, Mathematics & Music at Cornell University with a concentration in Programming Languages (PL). I'll be applying to graduate programs next cycle. I love research, teaching and building software! My interests lie in programming languages, compilers, formal verification and turning these into tools for building correct, efficient software.

Professionally, I work heavily within fullstack development. I also dabble in algorithms and competitive programming.

I am a strong believer that CS is at its best when used hand-in-hand with other areas. I am always searching new for ways to integrate fields ranging from music to ornithology!

Research & Work Experience

I research programming language theory, formal verification and computer architecture abstractions at CAPRA, where I am fortunate to be advised by Adrian Sampson and Anshuman Mohan. My research spans PL and computer networks, entering the fascinating realm of programmable packet scheduling. I work on developing programming languages & hardware for packet scheduling, with my specific research verifying the correctness of our compilers.

I am a senior member, developer & frontend lead on Cornell's CMSX research & project team, where I am fortunate to be advised by Andrew Myers and work alongside a team of fantastic colleagues. I primarily work on developing better systems for student-staff interaction and maximizing the efficiency of UI.

I worked for two years as a data analyst & software engineer at the Cornell Lab Of Ornithology, on the Merlin project.

Teaching Experience

I have spent two years as Head Teaching Assistant of Cornell's foundational course in functional programming, advanced data structures and programming langauges. I am fortunate to work with Michael Clarkson, under whose guidance I have coauthored multiple assignments, labs and recitation material, taught hundreds of students and led three generations of course staff.

I priorly served as the lead mentor & curriculum developer at CodeForFun's HackHighSchool program.

I have founded and tutored several educational programs in fundamentals of programming and computer science.

Projects

  • Franz – A compiled programming language targeted towards compact and modular musical composition, using a text-based musical notation. Compiles to ChucK, an audio production and representation language.

  • KLaTsL – A programming language for linear algebra. Explores the idea of a dimensional type system, and presents syntax including matrix comprehensions and typechecked transformations.

  • FindYourBird – A Bird ID and information targeted towards amateur ornithologists around California, allowing users to search, identify, read about and post information and photos about birds around them.

  • Saberchat – A platform targeted towards centralizing communities and their networks. Lets users quickly share & access information, and presents multiple features for community organizations.
